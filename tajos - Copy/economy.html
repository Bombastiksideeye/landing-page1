Economy of Ifugao Province

Where Timeless Traditions Meet Majestic Landscapes

The economy of Ifugao Province is primarily agricultural, with rice farming being the predominant economic activity.


  • Rice Terraces Agriculture: Ifugao is famous for its ancient rice terraces, which have been cultivated by the indigenous Ifugao people for over 2,000 years. These terraces are not only a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a tourist attraction but also the main source of livelihood for many residents. Rice, along with other crops such as vegetables, root crops, and fruits, is grown in the terraces using traditional farming methods.

  • Livestock and Poultry: In addition to crop farming, livestock and poultry raising also contribute to the economy of Ifugao Province. Farmers raise livestock such as pigs, chickens, and carabaos (water buffalo) for meat, milk, and other products.

  • Handicrafts and Weaving: The indigenous people of Ifugao are skilled in traditional handicrafts and weaving. They produce a variety of handicrafts such as baskets, mats, wood carvings, and traditional clothing, which are sold locally and to tourists.

  • Tourism: Tourism plays an increasingly significant role in the economy of Ifugao Province. The stunning landscapes, cultural heritage sites, and indigenous traditions attract visitors from around the world. Tourists visit the famous Banaue Rice Terraces, as well as other attractions such as hot springs, waterfalls, and historical sites.

  • Small-Scale Mining: Small-scale mining activities, particularly for gold and other minerals, also contribute to the economy of Ifugao. However, these activities are often informal and can have negative environmental and social impacts if not properly regulated.

  • Government Services: Government services and employment also contribute to the economy of Ifugao Province. Local government offices, schools, healthcare facilities, and other public services provide employment opportunities for residents.

Overall, the economy of Ifugao Province is diverse, with agriculture, tourism, handicrafts, and government services being the main drivers of economic activity. Efforts to promote sustainable agriculture, eco-tourism, and cultural preservation are important for the continued development and prosperity of the province.
