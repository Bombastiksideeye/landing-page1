Geography of Ifugao Province

The geography of Ifugao Province is characterized by its mountainous terrain, lush forests, and terraced rice fields. Here's some information about the geography of Ifugao:


  • Location: Ifugao Province is located in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) in the northern part of Luzon Island in the Philippines.

  • Topography: The province is predominantly mountainous, with the Cordillera Central mountain range running through its territory. The rugged terrain is interspersed with deep valleys and gorges, creating breathtaking landscapes.

  • Rice Terraces: Ifugao is famous for its ancient rice terraces, often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World." These terraces are meticulously carved into the mountainsides and have been cultivated by the Ifugao people for over 2,000 years.
  • Forests: Ifugao Province is home to diverse forests, including tropical rainforests and pine forests. These forests support a wide variety of flora and fauna, including endemic species.

  • Rivers and Waterfalls: Several rivers and waterfalls can be found in Ifugao, providing water for irrigation and hydroelectric power generation. Notable waterfalls include the Tappiyah Falls and the Pongas Falls.

  • Climate: Ifugao has a tropical climate, with relatively cool temperatures due to its high elevation. The province experiences two distinct seasons: the wet season from June to October and the dry season from November to May.

  • Biodiversity: Ifugao Province is rich in biodiversity, with a wide range of plant and animal species inhabiting its forests and mountains. Efforts are underway to conserve and protect the province's natural heritage.

Overall, the geography of Ifugao Province plays a significant role in shaping its cultural identity and economic activities, making it a fascinating destination for travelers and nature enthusiasts.
